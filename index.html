Hack Camp is a two-week summer program designed for high school students who want to learn to code and make things. We'll bring you from your first line of code to publishing your first project.

When is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp is two weeks long and we're running Hack Camp multiple times throughout the summer. There are multiple start dates between June 1st and August 10th (e.g. June 1st, June 15th, June 29th, etc.).

Where is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp will take place in downtown San Francisco in the heart of the city's technology scene. Exact location will be sent to applicants upon acceptance.

When are applications due?

Round 1 applications are due Saturday, May 23rd at 12pm PDT.

Who is it for?

Hack Camp is for any high school student who wants to learn how to create apps, build websites, or make games. We'll be teaching the fundamentals of web development, but the skills learned are transferable to game development and app development. We welcome girls and boys, ages 14-18, no prior experience necessary (though young adults with some experience are welcome as well, of course). Please bring your own laptop.

How much does it cost?

Hack Camp costs $595 for the two-week program. However, inclusivity is extremely important to us and we are offering full need-based financial aid (see the question on the application form).

How many other students will be there?

Each session of Hack Camp will vary in size between 4 and 12 students. The student-teacher ratio will vary between nearly 1:1 and 4:1. For the specifics for a session, please contact us at summer@hackedu.us.

What if I don't know how to code?

Perfect! Hack Camp is designed for beginners with little or no experience.

Who is teaching this?

Hack Camp instruction is led by the founders of hackEDU, Zach Latta and Jonathan Leung, with a combined 15 years of coding experience. Zach has multiple applications in the App Store with over 4 million users in aggregate and Jonathan grew PennApps, the original college hackathon, from 80 to over 1,000 participants and created and taught the curriculum at All Star Code.

FAQ

What do I need to bring?

You should bring your laptop, lunch, and a thirst for learning. Laptops will be provided upon request if you do not have access to one (contact us).

What if I already know how to code?

There's always more to learn—why not learn more surrounded by awesome makers and doers with similar interests?

What types of activities will students do outside of coding?

Students will participate in team bonding activities, tech company tours, hands-on hacking, and more!

What if I have any questions?

Please do not hesitate to contact us with any questions, comments, or suggestions! Just shoot us an email at summer@hackedu.us.
